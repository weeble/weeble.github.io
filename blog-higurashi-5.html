Pondering

Wow, this has been quite a rollercoaster. I think I managed to connect better with this episode. I enjoyed this second episode better, as everybody felt a bit more integrated, and Keiichi's relationships with Mion and Shion were more interesting. Plus the latter half felt like it had more talking and less Keiichi freaking out.

I guess the broad strokes of my theories are:

  • The Shion did it theory. Shion pretended to be Mion for the latter portion of the story.
  • The Keiichi fucked up theory. Keiichi did something much worse than sneaking into the storehouse and caused a chain of disasters.
  • The Sonozaki conspiracy theory. Mion and Shion are colaborating! They're both in on it.
  • The third Sonozaki theory. Mion and Shion are actually triplets and their sister is "the demon".
  • The demons are real theory. Demons are real and they possess people and force them to do awful things.
  • The Mion did it theory. Keiichi already told us what happened. We don't need a theory.
  • The Takano's not dead theory. It was not her body in the drum.
  • The nobody's dead theory. A large proportion of the deaths never happened.

Shion did it

If this isn't the answer, it's one obnoxious red herring. There have been too many bits and pieces hinting at this and never properly acknowledged. It feels like the most well-motivated theory: we have plenty of suggestion from the scrapbooks of a rift in the Sonozaki family, we know Mion and Shion live apart, there's a tension between them, Mion is the heir and their mother seems to have been kicked out of the family and had her demon stripped from her. Shion does not have strong ties with Rika and Satoko that would stop her from killing them, and it does make sense to kill Mion's friends to punish her. Additionally, Shion explicitly mentions the idea of somebody killing all your friends first and you last, so it's an idea the author wants us to have in mind.

It gives us a satisfying explanation for Shion disappearing when she did: she needed to become Mion full-time to get direct access to the heads of the Three Families: Oryou, Mayor Kimiyoshi and Rika.

The critical failing of this theory is that it can't readily explain how Mion and Shion interact in the catacombs. The Shion in the cage—who in this theory would have to be Mion—does nothing to disabuse Keiichi of the notion that she is Shion. If the voice-acting can be relied upon, she was clearly speaking in Shion's voice, something Mion has no reason to do. Unless that's just her afraid voice? Perhaps I need to go back and see what she said and what the others said to her in this scene.

Keiichi's not being fully honest with us about the events around the forbidden storehouse. Perhaps he overreacted to Tomitake and Takano? Perhaps there was an accident? Or maybe somebody else killed Tomitake and then they all got sucked into covering it up because they couldn't admit they were there?

Clearly, the main reason to believe this is Keiichi's strange flashback to that night. I'm struggling to see any other connections or hints here that tie this into the rest of the story.

Unlike the Shion did it theory, this can explain why, despite our suspicion that they have switched places, the terrified "Shion" doesn't warn Keiichi that it isn't Mion behind him.

Demons are real (and having a bad time)

Demons possess people. A demon really possessed Mion, just like she said. They certainly can't to whatever they want, they have specific goals and appear to have significant constraints. With the talk of hundreds of attempts, perhaps the demons are experiencing a sort of Groundhog Day scenario and it's not going their way. Maybe they have only limited interventions? Maybe they need people to do particular things before they are able to intervene? But their goals are nebulous. Do they really just want to murder everybody?

Mion did it

It's pretty heavily hinted that it was Mion in the forbidden storehouse. And Keiichi eventually comes to the conclusion that it was Mion talking to him every time on the phone, pretending to be Shion. Maybe we know a lot less about Shion than we think? So Mion had it in for her sister all along, and used Keiichi to hurt her, having given up on him when he neglected her with the doll. I struggle with this one. I don't really understand Mion's motivation well. And it feels very underwhelming. You can't just have the characters explain most of the facts of the mystery straight!

Takano's not dead

Nobody's dead

We never see bodies for Rika, Satoko, Oryou or Kimiyoshi. We have questionable identification for Takano. Mion and Shion disappear, with us only finding their supposed bodies some time after the fact. (Okay maybe they pulled out bodies from the tar pool? I'm not terribly sure.)

A very tenuous hint for this might be the takoyaki story. Keiichi discerns that the takoyaki seller is selling a stick of eight takoyaki balls, but seven of the balls have no octopus at all, while one has expensive, high quality octopus. Perhaps this means... seven fake deaths and one real death?!
